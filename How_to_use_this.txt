HOW TO USE THE WORKSHOP AI TOOL
================================

WHAT IT DOES
------------
The tool listens to a workshop in real time, transcribes speech every N minutes
(default: 5), and sends the transcript to an AI that identifies:

  - Knowledge gaps: things the group recognizes as lacking research evidence
  - Assumptions: things being taken for granted without evidence
  - Synthesis: a rolling summary of what themes have come up across the session
  - Topics: what has been talked about most
  - Disagreements: places where participants seem to conflict

All of this updates automatically on a browser dashboard at http://localhost:5001.

Secondary features: image generation (Gemini/DALL-E), an assumptions diagram
Excel export, a post-session chat with the transcript, and a custom summary
generator.


THE PIPELINE (WHAT ACTUALLY HAPPENS)
-------------------------------------
Microphone -> 5-min audio chunk -> Whisper transcription -> text file -> 
text sent to LLM analysis -> analysis is displayed in browser


The pipeline runs continuously. While audio chunk N+1 is being recorded, chunk N
is being transcribed and analyzed in a background thread. The UI checks every
60 seconds for new results.


STARTING A SESSION
------------------
1. Run: python launch.py (or python3 launch.py on Mac)
2. Browser opens to http://localhost:5001/settings after ~30 seconds
3. Configure settings, click Start session
4. Wait for the red REC indicator to appear -- recording has started
5. The first analysis result appears after the first full chunk completes

First session: the Whisper model downloads on first run (hundreds of MB to
over 1 GB). Budget 5-10 extra minutes.


SETTINGS THAT MATTER
---------------------
Language:           Must match what will be spoken. Affects both transcription
                    accuracy AND the language of the AI analysis output.

Whisper model:      mlx = Apple Silicon only
                    faster-whisper = Windows default, works everywhere
                    standard = needs a dedicated GPU

Model size:         Medium is the right default. Large is more accurate but
                    needs 16+ GB RAM and is 2-3x slower.

Analysis frequency: 5 min is the right balance. 1 min gives noisy, short-context
                    results, good for testing. 10 min gives broader context but slower feedback.

AI model:           Claude > Gemini for analysis quality.
                    Local models (phi, gemma) are noticeably weaker but private.

Analysis window:    Whole subsession = AI sees everything since the session start
                    Last 3 chunks = AI only sees the most recent content


THE SMALL GROUP FEATURE
------------------------
When the group splits into breakout groups, click Start small group in the
settings (gear icon). This starts a separate analysis context so the small-group
transcript is tracked separately from the main session. Clicking End small group
makes sure small group discussion stays behind, and new context starts.



WHAT IS RELIABLE
----------------
Audio recording: Solid. A 5-second overlap between chunks prevents speech at
chunk boundaries from being lost. Proper nouns and acronyms are the most error-prone part of transcription.
  Names of people, places, and organizations often get garbled. There are no speaker labels, so 
  AI cannot tell who said what, but it is not necessary in this context.

Whisper transcription (medium model): Good for clear speech in a quiet room.
Degrades with background noise, multiple simultaneous speakers, technical
jargon, proper nouns, and fast speech. Errors are visible in the Transcript tab. 
Faster-whisper has around 5-10 % lower accuracy than standard Whisper.
MLX-optimised whisper is as accurate as standard Whisper.

Synthesis: The 4-6 theme cards are generally an honest
summary of what was discussed. LLMs are good at summarising. 


WHAT IS LESS RELIABLE
----------------------
Assumptions: AI's judgment call, not an objective classification. 
Treat them as provocations to consider, not diagnoses.

Knowledge gaps: The hardest task, so the least reliable. The AI has to identify a knowledge gap in research. Problems:
  - AI identifies gaps itself, not just shows what participants have classified as knowledge gaps.
  - AI uses outdated training data and does not know everything, 
    so it often says that there is no research, even though there actually is.
  - The AI interprets "likely intended words" from transcription -- this corrects real
    errors but also introduces unreliable interpretations


Topics: Treat the frequency counts as rough rankings, not actual word counts --
the AI estimates them rather than counting occurrences.

Disagreements: Could be generic or repeating what other tabs have already shown. 
The AI sees one continuous block of text with no speaker labels. 
It has to infer disagreement from content alone, which
it might get wrong or miss entirely.


HOW TO INTERPRET THE OUTPUT CORRECTLY
---------------------------------------
DO:
  - Use knowledge gaps and assumptions as ideas for facilitator intervention
    ("AI flagged this -- does the group recognize it?"), not as ground truth.
  - Cross-check against the Transcript tab. The raw transcript is the most
    reliable output. Everything else is an interpretation of it. 
    You can download transcript as a plain text file and do your own analysis.
  - Pay attention to topics with high counts -- frequency is easier for the AI
    to estimate than subtlety.

DON'T:
  - Trust any specific claim without reading the corresponding
    part of the transcript. The AI can produce plausible-sounding outputs that
    nobody actually mentioned.
  - Treat assumptions as things participants consciously hold - AI is guessing intent.


THE STAR / SAVE FEATURE
------------------------
You can star individual insights (star icon next to each item) to save them.
Starred items persist in browser localStorage and are also saved to disk.
Stars survive page reloads but reset when a new session starts.
You can download all saved insights as a text file from the Summary tab.
